# Path 5, 30-minute tier: LLM evals and observability

Two short pieces that fix the operator framing of evaluation as a discipline, not as a checklist.

## 1. Hamel Husain, "Your AI product needs evals"

[`hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals`](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals)

Hamel's post is the shortest path to the operator framing of evaluation. Honest, opinionated, and grounded in shipped work. The key claim is that evals are the product discipline that survives the model upgrade, not a checkbox you tick at release time.

Why this slot: the operator-level conversation about LLM systems eventually arrives at "we need to take evals seriously". Reading this once short-circuits the discovery process.

## 2. Eugene Yan, "LLM evaluation patterns"

[`eugeneyan.com/writing/llm-patterns`](https://eugeneyan.com/writing/llm-patterns/)

Eugene's running collection of patterns. The closest thing the field has to a vocabulary reference. Read it against Hamel's post and you have the why plus the how.

Why this slot: Yan's pattern catalogue is the working vocabulary the rest of the eval literature borrows from.
