# Path 1, 3-hour tier: AI-supported engineering

Two longer reads that move you from observer to operator.

## 1. `awslabs/aidlc-workflows`, end to end

[`github.com/awslabs/aidlc-workflows`](https://github.com/awslabs/aidlc-workflows)

The Amazon framing this document extends. Read the repository's documentation, the example workflows, and the AWS Builder's Library companion essay. Pay particular attention to the boundaries Amazon draws around each phase. The operator perspective starts where those boundaries end.

Why this slot: knowing the primary source matters before reading any commentary on it. The repository is the canonical reference and the search-term anchor for the field.

## 2. Will Larson, *An Elegant Puzzle*, the engineering-org-design chapters

[`lethain.com/elegant-puzzle`](https://lethain.com/elegant-puzzle/)

Larson's book on engineering management at scale. Read the chapters on organisational design and on running effective engineering teams. The book is not about AI augmentation; it is about the questions AI augmentation is supposed to answer. Reading it before the AIDLC manifesto gives you the right frame.

Why this slot: most failures in AI-augmented engineering at firm scale are organisational, not technical. The book is the closest published treatment of the organisational shape AI augmentation runs through.
