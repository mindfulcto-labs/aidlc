# Path 3, 30-minute tier: agentic AI engineering

Two short pieces that fix the operator's mental model of what an agent is.

## 1. Anthropic, "Building effective agents"

[`anthropic.com/research/building-effective-agents`](https://www.anthropic.com/research/building-effective-agents)

The December 2024 essay. Short, opinionated, grounded in production patterns. The key contribution is the taxonomy of workflows-versus-agents and the case for starting with the simpler shape. The piece refuses agent-framework hype while staying practical.

Why this slot: every credible agentic-AI conversation eventually references this essay. Reading it first means the rest parses cleanly.

## 2. The LangGraph quickstart, top to bottom

[`langchain-ai.github.io/langgraph/tutorials`](https://langchain-ai.github.io/langgraph/tutorials/introduction/)

Pick LangGraph over the older LangChain framing because the state-machine model is closer to what production agentic work demands than the chain-and-callback shape. Type the quickstart, do not just read it. The point is muscle memory.

Why this slot: LangGraph is the de-facto reference framework for production agentic work in Python as of 2026. Reading the docs once gives you the shape; the substrate decision belongs to the 30-hour tier.
