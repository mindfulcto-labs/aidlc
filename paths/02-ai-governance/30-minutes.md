# Path 2, 30-minute tier: AI governance, operationalised

Two short pieces that anchor the regulatory shape without burying you in legal text.

## 1. ICO guidance on AI and data protection

[`ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/artificial-intelligence`](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/artificial-intelligence/)

The UK regulator's plain-English framing of AI risk. Read the opening sections, especially the parts on automated decision-making, transparency, and data protection impact assessments. Operator-readable, which is why it sits at the 30-minute tier and not at the 3-hour tier.

Why this slot: UK firms answer to the ICO. The ICO's own framing is the right anchor; secondary commentary on it can wait until you have read the source.

## 2. FutureOfLife.org's EU AI Act guide

[`artificialintelligenceact.eu/the-act`](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/the-act/)

The non-vendor, civil-society overview of the EU AI Act. The conformity-assessment shape and the calendar of obligation dates are the two things to take away. The August 2026 GPAI obligations are particularly load-bearing.

Why this slot: the AI Act is the regulation everything else compares to. Reading a clean overview before the Act itself is the cheapest path through.
