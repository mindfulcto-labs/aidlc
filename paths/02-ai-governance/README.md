# Path 2: AI governance, operationalised

The reading order for a Director or compliance partner who needs to make AI-augmented engineering audit-ready under the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, and the UK AI Cyber Code of Practice. The path is governance-on-the-floor, not governance-on-the-shelf.

## Audience

Engineering Directors and VPs under regulatory pressure. Heads of Information Security and Risk. CDIOs. Internal-audit and assurance partners who want a vocabulary that lines up with what their engineering counterparts actually do.

## 30 minutes

- *(in progress)* The ICO's guidance on AI and data protection, opening pages only. The UK regulator's plain English framing of AI risk is the right anchor for any UK-headquartered firm; non-UK readers will still recognise the structure.
- *(in progress)* FutureOfLife.org's EU AI Act timeline summary. Lays out the conformity assessment, GPAI, and obligation dates without the vendor noise.

## 3 hours

- *(in progress)* ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard, Annex A skim. Read controls A.6 through A.10 with a notebook open. The point is not to memorise the controls; it is to recognise the language when an auditor uses it.
- *(in progress)* NIST AI RMF Playbook. Cross-walk to the EU AI Act articles. The Playbook is the connective tissue that lets a UK or EU team work with US-headquartered partners without re-translating every conversation.

## 30 hours

- *(in progress)* ISACA CGEIT study material, governance lens only. The CGEIT framing is the most concrete version of governance accountability written for the IT-leader audience, and the way it splits "evaluate, direct, monitor" maps cleanly onto an AI portfolio.
- *(in progress)* Implement a posture statement for one AI system using [`compliance-as-code`](https://github.com/mindfulcto-labs/compliance-as-code). The exercise is not the standard; the exercise is what running the standard against a real system teaches you about both.

## Notes

The path is regulation-heavy on purpose. Vendor governance products will tell you what to buy. The path tells you what to read so that you do not have to take the vendor's framing on trust.

The "in progress" tags will resolve as the entries land. The path is published with explicit placeholders because the shape and audience are stable; the specific references are still being authored.
