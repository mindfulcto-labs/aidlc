# Path 2: AI governance, operationalised

The reading order for a Director or compliance partner who needs to make AI-augmented engineering audit-ready under the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, and the UK AI Cyber Code of Practice. Governance-on-the-floor, not governance-on-the-shelf.

## Audience

Engineering Directors and VPs under regulatory pressure. Heads of Information Security and Risk. CDIOs. Internal-audit and assurance partners who want a vocabulary that lines up with what their engineering counterparts actually do.

## Tiers

- **[30 minutes](./30-minutes.md)**: ICO guidance on AI + FutureOfLife.org's EU AI Act overview.
- **[3 hours](./3-hours.md)**: ISO/IEC 42001 Annex A skim + NIST AI RMF Playbook.
- **[30 hours](./30-hours.md)**: ISACA CGEIT study material + posture build using `compliance-as-code`.
- **[Notes](./notes.md)**: why the path looks the way it does, jurisdictional notes, open questions.

## What this path is not

A substitute for legal counsel. Operator-grade governance maps regulatory obligations onto engineering artefacts. Whether the firm's specific facts satisfy the regulation is a question for a lawyer.

Vendor product documentation. Vendor governance products will tell you what to buy. The path tells you what to read so you do not have to take the vendor's framing on trust.
