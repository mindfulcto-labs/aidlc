# Path 2: notes

## Why the path looks the way it does

Governance literature is split between high-level frameworks (great vocabulary, vague operationalisation) and vendor product documentation (great operationalisation, locked to one product). The path threads between the two by combining the regulator's own framing (ICO, FutureOfLife) with the standards proper (ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF) and one operator-grade implementation exercise (the `compliance-as-code` posture pack).

The path is jurisdiction-aware. The 30-minute tier leans UK; the 3-hour tier is EU and US; the 30-hour tier is the credential most operators in the target audience are already pursuing. If your jurisdiction differs materially, the principle holds but the specific entry list should adapt.

## Resources considered but not included

- The full EU AI Act text. A guided read in the 30-minute tier gives the operator what they actually need. Specialists will read the regulation; the path is not written for specialists.
- ISO/IEC 23894 (AI risk management). Strong companion to ISO 42001; adds rather than replaces; deferred.
- Vendor governance product documentation. Excluded for the reasons noted in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
