# Path 2, 3-hour tier: AI governance, operationalised

The standards proper.

## 1. ISO/IEC 42001:2023, Annex A skim

[`iso.org/standard/81230.html`](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html)

The AI management system standard. Read controls A.6 through A.10 with a notebook open. The point is not to memorise the controls; it is to recognise the language when an auditor uses it. The BSI shop sells the UK version; some content is available through national standards bodies' free-preview mechanisms.

Why this slot: ISO/IEC 42001 is the standard auditors will name when they assess your AI portfolio. Reading the controls once is the cheapest defence against being surprised in a meeting.

## 2. NIST AI Risk Management Framework Playbook

[`airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/Playbook`](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/Playbook)

NIST's companion to the AI RMF 1.0 framework. The Playbook does the operational translation that the standard avoids. Read GOVERN and MEASURE in full; skim MAP and MANAGE. Pair this with the ISO read by holding the two side-by-side on a few specific obligations (record-keeping, accuracy, human oversight). The mapping work survives long after specific clauses revise.

Why this slot: NIST's framing is the connective tissue between EU obligations and US partner expectations. Multi-jurisdictional AI work uses AI RMF as the shared vocabulary.
